Imagine an **axially symmetric magnet rotating at constant angular velocity about its axis** – a cylindrical bar magnet, if you like, or for that matter **the Earth itself (pretending that the magnetic axis coincides with the rotation axis)**. It is natural to wonder **whether its magnetic field is stationary, or rotates with the magnet** (or, as Arlo Guthrie would say, “Maybe it doesn’t even matter”). Are the field lines “dragged along” by the rotation of the source? 

地球是个巨大的磁体，地球沿着转轴自转，我们对于转动中的磁体会不会导致磁场发生变化

Faraday did a series of experiments (variations on the disk experiment of [[7.1 Electromotive Force#^04f99c|Ex. 7.4]]) that appeared to settle the issue. **Mounting the magnet along the axis of the disk he first held the magnet still while he rotated the disk** (that’s the original version), and **a current was generated in the circuit** (Fig. 7.51). 

![[imgs/rotating_magnet.png]]

Then he **allowed the magnet to rotate at the same rate as the disk**; again **a current was generated**. Finally, he **held the disk at rest and rotated the magnet**. This time **no current flowed**. <mark style="background: #ADCCFFA6;">He concluded that the field of the rotating magnet is the same as that of the stationary magnet</mark>: that’s why the first two experiments give the same result, and the third is just what you’d get if the magnet were at rest. 

> [!note] 
> Apparently **a magnet rotating about its axis of symmetry does not drag the field around** as it spins;

法拉第做了3个实验：

1. 转动转盘，固定磁铁 - 转盘中 **出现电流**
2. 同时转动转盘和磁铁 - 转盘中 **出现电流**
3. 固定转盘，转动磁铁 - 转盘中 **不出现电流**

因此他得到了 **沿着对称轴旋转** 的磁体的磁场不会随之旋转的结论。

And yet, if the magnet **rotates about an axis perpendicular to its axis of symmetry**, then it **certainly does drag the field along with it**. The field lines loop around from the north pole to the south pole, and that direction keeps changing. 

但是，如果不沿着对称轴旋转，磁场显然会随着磁铁的旋转而旋转。

So not everyone accepted Faraday’s conclusion, until 1912 when experiments by **S. J. Barnett** finally led to a consensus that Faraday was right – **in particular, the Earth’s magnetic field (or more precisely the part of it associated with rotation about the geographic north-south axis) does not rotate with the Earth**.

But what exactly does it mean to say that the field lines do (or do not) rotate with their source? Faraday had a very concrete conception of field lines as a network of “wires” (or cobwebs) existing “out there” in space. In that case, to say that they are moving (or not) makes perfect sense. But in Maxwell’s theory it is fields that have real physical existence, and field lines are just constructs we devise (by joining up the arrows that represent the fields themselves) to help us visualize (and draw) the fields. And we have already learned that even static magnetic field lines can be problematic ([[|Section 5.3.4]] #tobesolved ).
