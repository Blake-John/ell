# 4.1.1 电介质 (Dielectrics)

In this chapter, we shall **study electric fields in matter**. Matter, of course, comes in many varieties – solids, liquids, gases, metals, woods, glasses – and these substances do not all respond in the same way to electrostatic fields. 

Nevertheless, most everyday objects belong (at least, in good approximation) to one of two large classes: **conductors** and **insulators** (or **dielectrics**). 

We have already talked about **conductors**; these are substances that **contain an “unlimited” supply of charges that are free to move about through the material**. In practice, what this ordinarily means is that many of the **electrons (one or two per atom, in a typical metal) are not associated with any particular nucleus**, but roam around in response to applied fields. 

In **dielectrics**, by contrast, all **charges are attached to specific atoms or molecules** – they’re **on a tight leash, and all they can do is move a bit within the atom or molecule**. Such microscopic displacements are not as dramatic as the wholesale rearrangement of charge in a conductor, but their **cumulative effects account for the characteristic behavior of dielectric materials**. There are actually two principal mechanisms by which **electric fields can distort the charge distribution of a dielectric atom or molecule**: **stretching** and **rotating**. In the next two sections I’ll discuss these processes.

**电介质与导体的本质区别**

- **导体**：含有 **大量自由电荷**（如金属中的自由电子），在外加电场下可自由移动，导致电荷重新分布，最终电场在导体内为零。
- **电介质**：所有电荷 **被束缚在原子或分子内**，无法自由移动，但在外加电场下会发生 **微观位移或取向变化**，形成 **极化现象**。这种微观位移的累积效应决定了电介质的宏观行为

**电介质的极化机制**

电介质在电场中的极化主要通过两种机制实现：  

- **拉伸机制（Stretching）**：
    - 中性原子的原子核和电子云在外加电场下发生微小相对位移，形成诱导偶极子。
    - 偶极矩方向与电场方向一致，且在弱场下近似成正比关系（如 $p = \alpha E$，其中 $\alpha$ 为原子极化率）。
- **旋转机制（Rotating）**：
    - 极性分子（如 H₂O）具有固有偶极矩，在外加电场下受扭矩作用趋向与电场方向一致。
    - 热运动会阻碍完全对齐，导致极化程度与温度相关。

# 4.1.2 感应偶极子 (Induced Dipoles)

Although the atom **as a whole is electrically neutral**, there is a **positively charged core (the nucleus)** and a **negatively charged electron** cloud surrounding it. These two regions of charge within the atom are **influenced by the field**: the nucleus is pushed in the direction of the field, and the electrons the opposite way.

电介质在电场中整体呈现电中性，但是其原子的结构仍存在正负电荷，这些正负电荷会被外加电场影响。**原子核（正电）和电子云（负电）会发生微小相对位移**，形成诱导偶极子。因此尽管原子整体呈电中性，但 **正负电荷中心的分离导致偶极矩的产生**。

> [!tips]
> In principle, if the **field is large enough**, it can **pull the atom apart** completely, **ionizing** it (the substance then becomes a conductor).

With less extreme fields, however, an **equilibrium is soon established**, for if the center of the electron cloud does not coincide with the nucleus, these positive and negative charges attract one another, and that holds the atom together. 

The two opposing forces ($E$ **pulling the electrons and nucleus apart,** their **mutual attraction drawing them back together**) reach a **balance**, leaving the atom **⌈polarized⌋** #def/electrodynamics/matters, with plus charge shifted slightly one way, and minus the other. 

The atom now **has a tiny dipole moment $p$** , which **points in the same direction as $E$**. Typically, this induced dipole moment is **approximately proportional to the field** (as long as the latter is not too strong): $$p=\alpha E . \tag{4.1}$$ ^b08236

The constant of proportionality $\alpha$ is called **⌈atomic polarizability⌋** #def/electrodynamics/matters . Its value **depends on the detailed structure of the atom** in question. [[4.1 Polarization#^e628a5|Table 4.1]] lists some experimentally determined atomic polarizabilities.

电场力使正负电荷分离，而电荷间的吸引力阻止进一步分离，最终达到平衡状态。此时偶极矩方向与电场方向一致。且偶极矩与场强约为线性关系，其比例系数称为 **极化率** 。

![[imgs/polarizability.png]] ^e628a5

> [!example] Example 4.1. 
> A primitive model for an atom consists of a point nucleus ( $+q$ ) surrounded by a uniformly charged spherical cloud ( $-q$ ) of radius a (Fig. 4.1). Calculate the atomic polarizability of such an atom.
> 
> **Solution**. 
> In the presence of an external field E, the nucleus will be shifted slightly to the right and the electron cloud to the left, as shown in Fig. 4.2. (Because the actual displacements involved are extremely small, as you’ll find in Prob. 4.1, it is reasonable to assume that the electron cloud retains its spherical shape.) Say that equilibrium occurs when the nucleus is displaced a distance d from the center of the sphere. At that point, the external field pushing the nucleus to the right exactly balances the internal field pulling it to the left: $E=E_{e}$ , where $E_{e}$ is the field produced by the electron cloud. Now the field at a distance d from the center of a uniformly charged sphere is $$E_{e}=\frac{1}{4 \pi \epsilon_{0}} \frac{q d}{a^{3}}$$
> 
> ![[imgs/single_atom_polarize.png]]
> 
> (Prob. 2.13). At equilibrium, then, $$E=\frac{1}{4 \pi \epsilon_{0}} \frac{q d}{a^{3}}, or p=q d=\left(4 \pi \varepsilon_{0} a^{3}\right) E .$$
> The atomic polarizability is therefore $$\alpha=4 \pi \varepsilon_{0} a^{3}=3 \epsilon_{0} v, \tag{4.2}$$ where v is the volume of the atom. Although this atomic model is extremely crude, the result (Eq. 4.2) is not too bad – it’s accurate to within a factor of 4 or so for many simple atoms.

For **molecules the situation is not quite so simple**, because frequently **they polarize more readily in some directions than in others**. Carbon dioxide (Fig. 4.3), for instance, has a polarizability of $4.5 ×10^{-40} C^{2} ~m / N$ when you apply the field **along the axis of the molecule**, but only $2 ×10^{-40}$ for fields **perpendicular to this direction**. 

When the field is **at some angle to the axis**, you must **resolve it into parallel and perpendicular components**, and multiply each by the pertinent polarizability: $$p=\alpha_{\perp} E_{\perp}+\alpha_{\| } E_{\| } .$$

![[imgs/carbon_dioxide.png|400]]

In that case the **induced dipole moment may not even be in the same direction as $E$** .And $CO_{2}$ is relatively simple, as molecules go, since the atoms at least arrange themselves in a straight line; for a **completely asymmetrical molecule**, [[4.1 Polarization#^b08236|Eq. 4.1]] is replaced by the most general linear relation between $\mathbf{E}$ and $\mathbf{p}$: $$\left.\begin{array}{l} p_{x}=\alpha_{x x} E_{x}+\alpha_{x y} E_{y}+\alpha_{x z} E_{z}, \\ p_{y}=\alpha_{y x} E_{x}+\alpha_{y y} E_{y}+\alpha_{y z} E_{z}, \\ p_{z}=\alpha_{z x} E_{x}+\alpha_{z y} E_{y}+\alpha_{z z} E_{z} . \end{array}\right\}$$
The set of nine constants $\alpha_{i j}$ constitute the **⌈polarizability tensor⌋** #def/electrodynamics/matters  for the molecule. Their values **depend on the orientation of the axes you use**, though it is always possible to choose “principal” axes such that all the off-diagonal terms ( $\alpha_{x y}$ , $\alpha_{z x}$ , etc.) vanish, leaving just three nonzero polarizabilities: $\alpha_{x x}$ , $\alpha_{y y}$ , and $\alpha_{z z}$ 

对于分子而言，其极化往往更加复杂。若物体对称，我们还可以将场强分解为特定方向上的分量，并乘上该方向上的极化率。但是分子往往更加复杂，这个时候我们就需要考虑各个方向上的极化率，得到一个 **极化率张量** ，用该张量来描述偶极矩，即为 $$p_{i}​=\sum_{j}​\alpha_{ij}​E_{j} \qquad ​(i,j=x,y,z)$$
若写成矩阵形式，则有 $$\begin{bmatrix}p_{x}\\p_{y}\\p_{z}\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}
a_{xx} & a_{xy} & a_{xz} \\
a_{yx} & a_{yy} & a_{yz} \\
a_{zx} & a_{zy} & a_{zz}
\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}
E_{x}\\E_{y}\\E_{z}
\end{bmatrix}$$
# 4.1.3 极性分子的取向 (Alignment of Polar Molecules)

Some molecules **have built- in, permanent dipole moments**. In the water molecule, for example, the electrons tend to cluster around the oxygen atom (Fig. 4.4), and since the molecule is bent at $105\degree$, this **leaves a negative charge at the vertex and a net positive charge on the opposite side.** 

![[imgs/water_dipole.png]]

水分子由于其构型，本身就具有一定的极性。

What happens when such molecules (called **⌈polar molecules⌋** #def/electrodynamics/matters ) are **placed in an electric field**?

If the field is uniform, the force on the positive end, $F_{+}=q E$ , exactly cancels the force on the negative end, $F_{-}=-q E$ (Fig. 4.5). However, there will be a **torque**: $$\begin{aligned} N & =\left(r_{+} × F_{+}\right)+\left(r_{-} × F_{-}\right) \\ & =\left[ \left( \dfrac{d}{2} \right) \times(q E) \right]+\left[ \left( -\dfrac{d}{2} \right) \times(-q E) \right]=q\mathbf{d × E} . \end{aligned}$$ 

![[imgs/torque_on_water.png]]

Thus a dipole $p=q d$ in a uniform field E **experiences a torque** $$N=\mathbf{p} × \mathbf{E} . \tag{4.4}$$Notice that N is in such a direction as to **line $p$ up *parallel* to $E$** ; a polar molecule that is **free to rotate will swing around until it points in the direction of the applied field**. ^3774ed

在匀强电场中，作用在两级的力大小相等，只剩下一个力矩的作用效果， **使得偶极子与电场方向对齐**

If the field is **nonuniform**, so that $F_{+}$ does **not exactly balance** $F_{-}$ , there will be a **net force on the dipole, in addition to the torque**. Of course, $E$ **must change rather abruptly** for there to be significant variation in the space of one molecule, so this is not ordinarily a major consideration in discussing the behavior of dielectrics. Nevertheless, **the formula for the force on a dipole in a nonuniform field is of some interest**: $$F=F_{+}+F_{-}=q\left(E_{+}-E_{-}\right)=q(\Delta E),$$where $\Delta E$ represents the **difference between the field at the plus end and the field at the minus end**. Assuming the dipole is very short, we may use [[Eq. 1.35]] to approximate the small change in $E_{x}$ : $$\Delta E_{x} \equiv\left(\nabla E_{x}\right) \cdot d,$$with corresponding formulas for $E_{y}$ and $E_{z}$ . 

More compactly, $$\Delta E=(d \cdot \nabla) E,$$and therefore $$F=(p \cdot \nabla) E . \tag{4.5}$$For a “perfect”dipole of infinitesimal length, [[4.1 Polarization#^3774ed|Eq. 4.4]] **gives the torque about the center of the dipole even in a nonuniform field**; about any other point, $N=(p ×E)+(r ×F)$

在非匀强电场中，两力并不相等，此时作用在其上的力与场强的变化速度有关，而其力矩仍然由偶极矩和场强决定。

# 4.1.4 极化 (Polarization)

What happens to a piece of dielectric material when it is placed in an electric field?

If the substance consists of  **neutral atoms** (or **nonpolar molecules**), the field will induce in each a **tiny dipole moment**, pointing in the same direction as the field. If the material is made up of **polar molecules**, each permanent dipole will **experience a torque, tending to line it up along the field direction**. 

> [!seealso] 
> **Random thermal motions** *compete* with this process, so the **alignment is never complete**, especially at higher temperatures, and disappears almost at once when the field is removed.

Notice that these two mechanisms produce the same basic result: **a lot of little dipoles pointing along the direction of the field** – the material becomes **⌈polarized⌋** #def/electrodynamics/matters . A convenient measure of this effect is $$\mathbf{P} \equiv dipole\ moment\ per\ unit\ volume,$$which is called the **⌈polarization⌋** #def/electrodynamics/matters . 

电介质在 **电场** 作用下，内部**微观电荷（正负电荷中心）发生相对位移或偶极子取向排列**，导致宏观偶极矩的现象。

极化强度 $\mathbf{P}$ 是描述电介质宏观极化程度的核心物理量，其方向与电场方向一致（对于线性电介质）。

Even in polar molecules there will be some polarization by displacement (though generally it is a lot easier to rotate a molecule than to stretch it, so the second mechanism dominates). **It’s even possible in some materials to “freeze in” polarization, so that it persists after the field is removed**. 

**两种机制**：
- **位移极化（Displacement Polarization）**：  
	- 非极性分子（如 CO₂）或中性原子在电场中，正负电荷中心分离形成诱导偶极子（如 [[4.1 Polarization#4.1.2 感应偶极子 (Induced Dipoles)|4.1.2]] 节所述）。
- **取向极化（Orientation Polarization）**：  
	- 极性分子（如 H₂O）固有偶极矩在外加电场下趋向与电场方向一致（如 [[4.1 Polarization#4.1.3 极性分子的取向 (Alignment of Polar Molecules)|4.1.3]] 节所述）。

But let’s forget for a moment about the cause of the polarization; **we’re going to study the field that a chunk of polarized material itself produces**. Then, in Section 4.3, we’ll put it all together: the original field, which was responsible for P, plus the new field, which is due to P.
