# 1.1.1 What is Vectors ?

## Definition

> [!PDF|note] [01 Vector Analysis, p.1](Physics/Electrodynamics/chapters/01%20Vector%20Analysis.pdf#page=1&selection=15,7,29,47&color=note)
> >  **displacements** (straight line segments going from one point to another) have **direction** as well as **magnitude** (length), and it is essential to take both into account when you combine them

> [!PDF|def] [01 Vector Analysis, p.1](Physics/Electrodynamics/chapters/01%20Vector%20Analysis.pdf#page=1&selection=29,65,32,1&color=def)
> >  called **vectors**:
> 
> 矢量有大小和方向

> [!PDF|def] [01 Vector Analysis, p.1](Physics/Electrodynamics/chapters/01%20Vector%20Analysis.pdf#page=1&selection=33,10,35,7&color=def)
> > quantities that **have magnitude** but **no direction** are called scalars
> 
> 标量只有大小没有方向

## Propertities
### Magnitude

> [!PDF|def] [01 Vector Analysis, p.1](Physics/Electrodynamics/chapters/01%20Vector%20Analysis.pdf#page=1&selection=48,17,61,1&color=def)
> > **magnitude** of a vector A is written |A| or, more simply, *A*.
> 
> 称为 **模**

### Locations

> [!PDF|important] [01 Vector Analysis, p.1](Physics/Electrodynamics/chapters/01%20Vector%20Analysis.pdf#page=1&selection=76,39,80,0&color=important)
> > Note that vectors have magnitude and direction but **not location**
> 
> 矢量没有位置，可以平移

## Calculations

### 1 Addition

> [!PDF|def] [01 Vector Analysis, p.2](Physics/Electrodynamics/chapters/01%20Vector%20Analysis.pdf#page=2&selection=59,0,81,10&color=def)
> > Place the tail of B at the head of A; the sum, A + B, is the vector **from the tail of A to the head** of B (Fig. 1.3)
> 
> 首尾相连

![01 Vector Analysis, p.2|500](Physics/Electrodynamics/chapters/01%20Vector%20Analysis.pdf#page=2&rect=155,549,317,665&color=def)

#### commutative

> ([01 Vector Analysis, p.2](Physics/Electrodynamics/chapters/01%20Vector%20Analysis.pdf#page=2&selection=83,0,85,12&color=note))
> Addition is **commutative**:

$$ \textbf{A + B = B + A}$$

#### associative

> ([01 Vector Analysis, p.2](Physics/Electrodynamics/chapters/01%20Vector%20Analysis.pdf#page=2&selection=102,31,104,12&color=note))
>  Addition is also **associative**:

$$\textbf{(A + B) + C = A + (B + C)}$$

### 2 Multiplication by a scalar

> [!PDF|def] [01 Vector Analysis, p.2](Physics/Electrodynamics/chapters/01%20Vector%20Analysis.pdf#page=2&selection=154,0,164,11&color=def)
> > Multiplication of a vector by a positive scalar a **multiplies the magnitude** but leaves the **direction unchanged** (Fig. 1.5).
> 
> 模长改变方向不变

![01 Vector Analysis, p.2|400](Physics/Electrodynamics/chapters/01%20Vector%20Analysis.pdf#page=2&rect=156,414,285,534&color=def)

#### distributive

> ([01 Vector Analysis, p.2](Physics/Electrodynamics/chapters/01%20Vector%20Analysis.pdf#page=2&selection=168,40,171,13&color=note))
>  Scalar multiplication is **distributive**:

$$a(\mathbf{A + B}) = a\mathbf{A} + a\mathbf{B}$$

### Dot product

> [!PDF|def] [01 Vector Analysis, p.2](Physics/Electrodynamics/chapters/01%20Vector%20Analysis.pdf#page=2&selection=192,6,211,0&color=def)
> > Dot product of two vectors. The dot product of two vectors is defined by 
> 
> $$\mathbf{A} \cdot \mathbf{B} = AB\cos{\theta}$$
> 
> > [!PDF|def] [01 Vector Analysis, p.3](Physics/Electrodynamics/chapters/01%20Vector%20Analysis.pdf#page=3&selection=4,0,8,57&color=def)
> where θ is the angle they form when placed tail to tail (Fig. 1.6)

![01 Vector Analysis, p.2|400](Physics/Electrodynamics/chapters/01%20Vector%20Analysis.pdf#page=2&rect=316,414,425,523&color=def)

> [!PDF|important] [01 Vector Analysis, p.3](Physics/Electrodynamics/chapters/01%20Vector%20Analysis.pdf#page=3&selection=8,60,23,1&color=important)
> Note that $\mathbf{A} \cdot \mathbf{}{B}$ is itself **a scalar** (hence the alternative name **scalar product**)
> 
> 点乘得矢量

#### properties

> ([01 Vector Analysis, p.3](Physics/Electrodynamics/chapters/01%20Vector%20Analysis.pdf#page=3&selection=23,3,27,0&color=note))
> The dot product is **commutative**

$$\mathbf{A} \cdot \mathbf{B} = \mathbf{B} \cdot \mathbf{A}$$

> ([01 Vector Analysis, p.3](Physics/Electrodynamics/chapters/01%20Vector%20Analysis.pdf#page=3&selection=45,0,46,0&color=note))
> also **distributive**

$$\mathbf{A} \cdot (\mathbf{B + C}) = \mathbf{A} \cdot \mathbf{B} + \mathbf{A} \cdot \mathbf{C}$$

#### projection

> [!PDF|note] [01 Vector Analysis, p.3](Physics/Electrodynamics/chapters/01%20Vector%20Analysis.pdf#page=3&selection=78,0,109,2&color=note)
> > Geometrically, A · B is the product of **A times the projection of B along A** (or the product of B times the projection of A along B).
> 
> 点乘是 **投影之积**
> 
> 其几何意义在于，其值为两个向量组成的 **平行四边形的面积**

> [!PDF|note] [01 Vector Analysis, p.3](Physics/Electrodynamics/chapters/01%20Vector%20Analysis.pdf#page=3&selection=109,3,124,1&color=note)
> > If the two vectors are **parallel**, then A · B = AB. In particular, for any vector A,
> 
> $$\mathbf{A} \cdot A = A^{2}$$

> [!PDF|note] [01 Vector Analysis, p.3](Physics/Electrodynamics/chapters/01%20Vector%20Analysis.pdf#page=3&selection=140,0,158,1&color=note)
> If A and B are **perpendicular**, then $\mathbf{A} \cdot \mathbf{B} = 0$



> [!example] [01 Vector Analysis, p.3|500](Physics/Electrodynamics/chapters/01%20Vector%20Analysis.pdf#page=3&rect=128,321,446,461&color=example) 
> **Example 1.1.** Let $C = A - B$ (Fig. 1.7), and calculate the dot product of $C$ with itself. 
>*Solution* 
> $$ \begin{align*} C\cdot C&=(A - B)\cdot(A - B)\\ &=A\cdot A - A\cdot B - B\cdot A + B\cdot B \end{align*} $$
> or 
> $$C^{2}=A^{2}+B^{2}-2AB\cos\theta$$
> This is the **law of cosines**.
> 
> ![[Physics/Electrodynamics/chapters/01 Vector Analysis.pdf#page=4&rect=152,562,267,666|01 Vector Analysis, p.4|400]]

### Cross product

> [!PDF|def] [01 Vector Analysis, p.3](Physics/Electrodynamics/chapters/01%20Vector%20Analysis.pdf#page=3&selection=270,5,273,10&color=def)
> > Cross product of two vectors. The cross product of two vectors is defined by
> 
> $$\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B} = AB\sin{\theta}\mathbf{\hat{n}}$$
> 
> > [!PDF|def] [01 Vector Analysis, p.3](Physics/Electrodynamics/chapters/01%20Vector%20Analysis.pdf#page=3&selection=294,0,311,1&color=def)
> > where $\mathbf{\hat{n}}$ is a **unit vector** (vector of magnitude 1) pointing **perpendicular to the plane of A and B**.

> [!PDF|note] [01 Vector Analysis, p.3](Physics/Electrodynamics/chapters/01%20Vector%20Analysis.pdf#page=3&selection=318,55,352,13&color=note)
> > The ambiguity is resolved by the **right-hand rule**: let your fingers **point in the direction of the first vector and curl around (via the smaller angle) toward the second**; then your **thumb indicates the direction** of $\mathbf{\hat{n}}$. (In Fig. 1.8, A × B points into the page; B × A points out of the page.)
> 
> 顺着两个向量绕手，拇指得法向

> [!PDF|important] [01 Vector Analysis, p.3](Physics/Electrodynamics/chapters/01%20Vector%20Analysis.pdf#page=3&selection=352,14,368,2&color=important)
> > Note that $\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B}$ is itself **a vector** (hence the alternative name **vector product**).
> 
> 叉乘得 **矢量** 

#### properties

> [!PDF|note] [01 Vector Analysis, p.3](Physics/Electrodynamics/chapters/01%20Vector%20Analysis.pdf#page=3&selection=368,7,370,12&color=note)
> > cross product is **distributive**
> 
> $$\mathbf{A} \times (\mathbf{B + C}) = (\mathbf{A \times B} + \mathbf{A \times C})$$

> [!PDF|important] [01 Vector Analysis, p.3](Physics/Electrodynamics/chapters/01%20Vector%20Analysis.pdf#page=3&selection=407,0,410,10&color=important)
> > but **not commutative**. In fact,
> 
> $$(\mathbf{B \times A}) = -(\mathbf{A \times B})$$

> [!PDF|note] [01 Vector Analysis, p.4](Physics/Electrodynamics/chapters/01%20Vector%20Analysis.pdf#page=4&selection=19,0,37,11&color=note)
> Geometrically, |A × B| is **the area of the parallelogram** generated by A and B (Fig. 1.8).
> 

![01 Vector Analysis, p.4|400](Physics/Electrodynamics/chapters/01%20Vector%20Analysis.pdf#page=4&rect=282,562,425,668&color=note)

> [!PDF|note] [01 Vector Analysis, p.4](Physics/Electrodynamics/chapters/01%20Vector%20Analysis.pdf#page=4&selection=37,13,38,10&color=note)
> > if two vectors are parallel, their cross product is zero. In particular
> 
> $$\mathbf{A \times A} = \mathbf{0}$$
> 
> > [!PDF|note] [01 Vector Analysis, p.4](Physics/Electrodynamics/chapters/01%20Vector%20Analysis.pdf#page=4&selection=50,0,60,19&color=note)
> > for any vector A. (Here 0 is the zero vector, with magnitude 0.
> 
> **0矢量也是矢量** ， 既有大小也有方向

# 1.1.2 Component Form

## Definition

> [!PDF|note] [01 Vector Analysis, p.4](Physics/Electrodynamics/chapters/01%20Vector%20Analysis.pdf#page=4&selection=114,58,128,1&color=note)
> >  In practice, it is often easier to set up **Cartesian coordinates** x, y, z and work with vector components.
> 
> 使用 **笛卡尔坐标系** 运算更为简单

> [!PDF|def] [01 Vector Analysis, p.4](Physics/Electrodynamics/chapters/01%20Vector%20Analysis.pdf#page=4&selection=151,25,160,12&color=def)
> >  An arbitrary vector A can be expressed in terms of these basis vectors (Fig. 1.9b):
> 
> $$\mathbf{A} = A_{x}\mathbf{\hat{x}} + A_{y}\mathbf{\hat{y}} + A_{z}\mathbf{\hat{z}}$$
> 
> > [!PDF|def] [01 Vector Analysis, p.4](Physics/Electrodynamics/chapters/01%20Vector%20Analysis.pdf#page=4&selection=189,0,212,30&color=def)
> > The coefficients Ax, A y, and A z are the “**components**” of A; geometrically, they are the **projections of A along the three coordinate axes**
> 
> $$\begin{array}{l} A_{x} = \mathbf{A \cdot \hat{x}} \\ A_{y} = \mathbf{A \cdot \hat{y}} \\ A_{z} = \mathbf{A \cdot \hat{z}} \end{array}$$

![01 Vector Analysis, p.5](Physics/Electrodynamics/chapters/01%20Vector%20Analysis.pdf#page=5&rect=131,550,431,669&color=def)

## Rules
### rule1

> [!PDF|note] [01 Vector Analysis, p.5](Physics/Electrodynamics/chapters/01%20Vector%20Analysis.pdf#page=5&selection=44,1,81,0&color=note)
> > To add vectors, **add like components**: 
> 
>$$a \mathbf{A}=\left(a A_{x}\right) \mathbf{\hat{x}}+\left(a A_{y}\right) \mathbf{\hat{y}}+\left(a A_{z}\right) \mathbf{\hat{z}} .$$

### rule2

> [!PDF|note] [01 Vector Analysis, p.5](Physics/Electrodynamics/chapters/01%20Vector%20Analysis.pdf#page=5&selection=86,0,165,2&color=note)
> > To multiply by a scalar, **multiply each component**. Because ˆx, ˆy, and ˆz are mutually perpendicular unit vectors, 
> 
> $$\hat{x} \cdot \hat{x}=\hat{y} \cdot \hat{y}=\hat{z} \cdot \hat{z}=1, \hat{x} \cdot \hat{y}=\hat{x} \cdot \hat{z}=\hat{y} \cdot \hat{z}=0 .$$

> [!PDF|example] [01 Vector Analysis, p.5](Physics/Electrodynamics/chapters/01%20Vector%20Analysis.pdf#page=5&selection=168,0,254,1&color=example)
> > Accordingly,   $$\begin{aligned} A \cdot B & =\left(A_{x} \hat{x}+A_{y} \hat{y}+A_{z} \hat{z}\right) \cdot\left(B_{x} \hat{x}+B_{y} \hat{y}+B_{z} \hat{z}\right) \\ & =A_{x} B_{x}+A_{y} B_{y}+A_{z} B_{z} . \end{aligned}$$
> 
> 分量积之和

### rule3

> [!PDF|note] [01 Vector Analysis, p.5](Physics/Electrodynamics/chapters/01%20Vector%20Analysis.pdf#page=5&selection=259,0,290,1&color=note)
> > To calculate the dot product, multiply like components, and add. In particular, $$A \cdot A=A_{x}^{2}+A_{y}^{2}+A_{z}^{2},$$
> > so $$A=\sqrt{A_{x}^{2}+A_{y}^{2}+A_{z}^{2}} . $$ 
> > (This is, if you like, the three-dimensional generalization of the Pythagorean theorem.)

> [!PDF|note] [01 Vector Analysis, p.5](Physics/Electrodynamics/chapters/01%20Vector%20Analysis.pdf#page=5&selection=346,0,450,1&color=note)
> since
> > $$\begin{aligned} & \hat{x} × \hat{x}=\hat{y} × \hat{y}=\hat{z} × \hat{z}=0, \\ & \hat{x} × \hat{y}=-\hat{y} × \hat{x}=\hat{z}, \\ & \hat{y} × \hat{z}=-\hat{z} × \hat{y}=\hat{x}, \\ & \hat{z} × \hat{x}=-\hat{x} × \hat{z}=\hat{y} . \end{aligned}$$
>
> > [!PDF|note] [01 Vector Analysis, p.5](Physics/Electrodynamics/chapters/01%20Vector%20Analysis.pdf#page=5&selection=453,0,578,0&color=note)
> > Therefore, $$\begin{aligned} A × B & =\left(A_{x} \hat{x}+A_{y} \hat{y}+A_{z} \hat{z}\right) \times\left(B_{x} \hat{x}+B_{y} \hat{y}+B_{z} \hat{z}\right) \\ & =\left(A_{y} B_{z}-A_{z} B_{y}\right) \hat{x}+\left(A_{z} B_{x}-A_{x} B_{z}\right) \hat{y}+\left(A_{x} B_{y}-A_{y} B_{x}\right) \hat{z} . \end{aligned}$$

### rule4

> [!PDF|note] [01 Vector Analysis, p.6](Physics/Electrodynamics/chapters/01%20Vector%20Analysis.pdf#page=6&selection=4,0,4,71&color=note)
> > This cumbersome expression can be written more neatly as a determinant:$$A × B=\left|\begin{array}{ccc} \hat{x} & \hat{y} & \hat{z} \\ A_{x} & A_{y} & A_{z} \\ B_{x} & B_{y} & B_{z}\end{array}\right| .$$
> 
> [01 Vector Analysis, p.6](Physics/Electrodynamics/chapters/01%20Vector%20Analysis.pdf#page=6&rect=239,596,345,643&color=note)

> [!PDF|note] [01 Vector Analysis, p.6](Physics/Electrodynamics/chapters/01%20Vector%20Analysis.pdf#page=6&selection=67,0,88,0&color=note)
> > To calculate the cross product, form the determinant whose first row is $\hat{x}, \hat{y}, \hat{z}$ , whose second row is A (in component form), and whose third row is B.
> 
> 叉乘可以写成 **伪行列式形式**

# 1.1.3 Triple Products

## 1 Scalar Triple Product

> [!PDF|note] [01 Vector Analysis, p.7](Physics/Electrodynamics/chapters/01%20Vector%20Analysis.pdf#page=7&selection=52,2,99,38&color=note)
> > (i) Scalar triple product: $A \cdot(B ×C)$ . Geometrically, $|A \cdot(B ×C)|$ is the volume of the parallelepiped generated by A, B, and C, since $|B ×C|$ is the area of the base, and $|A cos\theta |$ is the altitude (Fig. 1.12). Evidently, $$A \cdot(B × C)=B \cdot(C × A)=C \cdot(A × B)$$ for they all correspond to the same figure. Note that **“alphabetical” order is preserved** – in view of Eq. 1.6, the “nonalphabetical” triple products $$A \cdot(C × B)=B \cdot(A × C)=C \cdot(B × A)$$ have the opposite sign.
> 
> 混合积的几何意义在于，其值为三个向量组成的 **平行六面体的体积**
> 
> 混合积具有 **顺序轮换性** ，否则会产生一个负号

![01 Vector Analysis, p.7|400](Physics/Electrodynamics/chapters/01%20Vector%20Analysis.pdf#page=7&rect=295,541,413,660&color=note)

> [!PDF|note] [01 Vector Analysis, p.7](Physics/Electrodynamics/chapters/01%20Vector%20Analysis.pdf#page=7&selection=186,23,186,41&color=note)
> > In component form, $$A \cdot(B × C)=\left|\begin{array}{lll}A_{x} & A_{y} & A_{z} \\ B_{x} & B_{y} & B_{z} \\ C_{x} & C_{y} & C_{z}\end{array}\right| . $$
> 
> [01 Vector Analysis, p.7](Physics/Electrodynamics/chapters/01%20Vector%20Analysis.pdf#page=7&rect=224,291,349,338&color=note)

> [!PDF|important] [01 Vector Analysis, p.7](Physics/Electrodynamics/chapters/01%20Vector%20Analysis.pdf#page=7&selection=254,14,254,47&color=note)
> > dot and cross can be **interchanged**
> 
> $$\mathbf{A \cdot (B \times C)} = \mathbf{(A \times B) \cdot C}$$

> [!PDF|important] [01 Vector Analysis, p.7](Physics/Electrodynamics/chapters/01%20Vector%20Analysis.pdf#page=7&selection=282,42,302,12&color=important)
> > however, the placement of the parentheses is critical: $(A \cdot B) \times C$ is a **meaningless expression** – you **can’t make a cross product from a scalar and a vector**
> 
> **数无法和向量做叉乘**

## 2 Vector Triple Product

> [!PDF|def] [01 Vector Analysis, p.7](Physics/Electrodynamics/chapters/01%20Vector%20Analysis.pdf#page=7&selection=307,0,322,1&color=def)
> > A × (B × C). The vector triple product can be simplified by the so-called **BAC–CAB rule**:
> 
> $$\mathbf{A \times (B \times C) = B(A \cdot C) - C(A\cdot B)}$$
> 
> [[Physics/Electrodynamics/chapters/01 Vector Analysis.pdf#page=7&rect=206,169,366,187&color=def|01 Vector Analysis, p.7]]

> [!PDF|def] [[Physics/Electrodynamics/chapters/01 Vector Analysis.pdf#page=7&selection=412,0,412,69&color=def|01 Vector Analysis, p.7]]
> $$(A × B) × C=-C \times(A × B)=-A(B \cdot C)+B(A \cdot C)$$
> 
> > is an entirely different vector (cross products are **not associative**).

# 1.1.4 Two Symbols and one rule

## 1. Kronecker symbol

### 定义

克罗内克符号通常用表示，其中和是整数或表示指标的变量，其定义为：  

$$\delta_{ij} = \left\{
\begin{array}{l}
1, \quad i = j \\
0, \quad i \neq j
\end{array}\right.
$$
例如 $\delta_{11} = 1, \delta_{12} = 0$ 等。

当 $i, j = x, y, z$ 时，我们通常将有

$$(\delta_{ij}) = \begin{bmatrix}
1 && 0 && 0 \\
0 && 1 && 0 \\
0 && 0 && 1
\end{bmatrix} = I$$

其中 $I$ 为 ***单位矩阵*** ，其元素为 $I_{ij} = \delta_{ij}$ 

## 2. 爱因斯坦求和约定

### 定义

- **重复指标求和**：当一个表达式中同一个指标（下标或上标）在同一个项内出现两次时，就默认要对该指标的所有可能取值进行求和，**无需显式写出求和符号** $\sum$ 。例如，在三维空间中，若有向量 $a_{i}$ 和 $b_{i}$，它们的点积按照常规写法是 $c = \sum_{i = 1}^{3}{a_{i}b_{i}}$ ，使用爱因斯坦求和约定则可简写成 $c = a_{i}b_{i}$ 。
- **哑指标**：上述重复出现用于求和的指标叫做 **哑指标**，简称哑标。表示哑标的小写字母可以用另一对小写字母替换，只要其取值范围不变。比如 $a_{i}b_{i}$ 也可以写成 $a_{j}b_{j}$ ，它们的含义是一样的，都表示对或的取值范围进行求和。
- **自由指标**：如果一个指标在一个表达式中只出现一次，它表示该指标是自由的，不需要求和，称为自由标。例如在中 $c_{i} = a_{ij}b_{j}$ ， $i$ 是自由标，表示结果 $c$ 的分量用 $i$ 标记， $j$ 是重复下标，表示要对 $j$ 进行求和。

### 应用

**矩阵乘法**：对于矩阵和相乘得到矩阵，常规写法是 $C_{ij} = \sum_{k = 1}^{3}{A_{ik}B_{kj}}$ ，采用爱因斯坦求和约定可写成 $C_{ij} = A_{ik}B_{kj}$ ，这里是 $k$ 哑标， $i$ 和 $j$ 是自由标，分别表示矩阵的行和列。

> [!example] 
> $$\delta_{ii} = \delta_{11} + \delta_{22} + \delta_{33 = 3}$$
> $$A_{ii} = A_{11} + A_{22} + A_{33} = \mathrm{Tr}(A)$$

## 3. Levi-Civita symbol

### 定义与取值规则
 
 三维空间中的列维 - 奇维塔符号通常用 $\varepsilon_{ijk}$ 表示，其中 $i, j, k$ 分别代表三维空间中的坐标轴方向，取值为 1, 2, 3。 

- **偶排列取值为1**：当 $(i, j, k)$ 是 $(1, 2, 3)$ 的偶排列时， $\varepsilon_{ijk}=1$ 。例如 $(1, 2, 3), (2, 3, 1), (3, 1, 2)$ 这几种排列都是偶排列，所以 $\varepsilon_{123}=\varepsilon_{231}=\varepsilon_{312}=1$ 。
- **奇排列取值为-1**：当 $(i, j, k)$ 是 $(1, 2, 3)$ 的奇排列时，$\varepsilon_{ijk}=-1$ 。例如 $(1, 3, 2), (2, 1, 3), (3, 2, 1)$ 是奇排列，即 $\varepsilon_{132}=\varepsilon_{213}=\varepsilon_{321}=-1$ 。
- **有重复元素取值为0**：当 $i, j, k$ 中有重复元素时，$\varepsilon_{ijk}=0$ 。比如$\varepsilon_{112}=\varepsilon_{223}=\varepsilon_{331}=\varepsilon_{111}=\varepsilon_{222}=\varepsilon_{333}=0$ 等。 

$$
\varepsilon_{ijk} = \left\{\begin{array}{l}
1, \quad ijk = 123, 231, 312 \\
-1, \quad ijk = 321, 213, 132 \\
0, \quad i = j\ or\ i = k\ or\ j = k
\end{array}\right.
$$

顺序为正，逆序为负，重复为零

> [!tip] 
> 如何理解奇偶排列？
> 这是一个线性代数的问题，当从 123 出发时，经过 **偶数次数字交换** 得到的排列称为偶排列，经过 **奇数次数字交换** 得到的排列称为奇排列。
