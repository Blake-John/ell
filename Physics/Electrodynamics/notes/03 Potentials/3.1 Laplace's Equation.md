# 3.1.1 Introduction

## 1. 静电学的核心任务

The **primary task** of electrostatics is to find the electric field of a given  
stationary charge distribution.

In principle, this purpose is accomplished by Coulomb’s law, in the form of

$$\mathbf{E(r)} = \dfrac{1}{4\pi \varepsilon_{0}}\int \dfrac{\hat{\xi}}{\xi}\rho(\mathbf{r'})\mathrm{d\tau'} \tag{3.1.1}$$

静电学的 **主要任务** 是确定给定静止电荷分布产生的电场。理论上，上式是基础。但实际应用中，该积分仅对简单电荷分布（如点电荷、均匀带电球体等）易于计算，**复杂情况（如导体表面电荷分布）则难以直接求解**。

Unfortunately, integrals of this type can be **difficult** to calculate for any but the simplest charge configurations.

## 2. 电势的引入

Occasionally we can get around this by **exploiting symmetry and using Gauss’s law**, but ordinarily the **best strategy is first to calculate the potential, V,** which is given by the somewhat more tractable equation.

为简化计算，通常先计算电势 V，其定义式（式 2.29）为：  

$$V(r) = \frac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0} \int \frac{1}{r} \rho(r') \, d\tau' \tag{3.2}$$

尽管电势的积分形式比电场更易处理，但在复杂问题中（如涉及导体时），电荷密度 $\rho$ 可能 **未知**，因为导体上的电荷会自由移动，仅能控制总电荷或电势。

The only thing we control directly is the **total charge** (or perhaps the **potential**) of each conductor.

#### 3. **泊松方程与拉普拉斯方程**

当电荷分布 $\rho$ 已知时，电势满足泊松方程：

$$\nabla^2 V = -\frac{\rho}{\varepsilon_0} \tag{3.3}$$

Very often, in fact, we are interested in **finding the potential in a region where ρ = 0**. (If ρ = 0 everywhere, of course, then V = 0, and there is nothing further to say – that’s not what I mean. There **may be plenty of charge elsewhere**, but we’re **confining our attention to places where there is no charge**.)

若研究区域内无电荷（$\rho = 0$），泊松方程简化为拉普拉斯方程： 

$$\nabla^2 V = 0 \tag{3.4}$$ 

在 **笛卡尔坐标系** 中展开为： 

$$\frac{\partial^2 V}{\partial x^2} + \frac{\partial^2 V}{\partial y^2} + \frac{\partial^2 V}{\partial z^2} = 0 \tag{3.5}$$

## 4. **拉普拉斯方程的适用场景**

- **无电荷区域**：即使整体存在电荷，只要关注区域内 $\rho = 0$，即可用拉普拉斯方程。例如，导体内部电场为零（$\rho = 0$），电势满足拉普拉斯方程。
- **边界条件**：方程需结合边界条件（如电势值或导数）才能唯一确定解，这引出了后续的唯一性定理和求解方法（如分离变量法、镜像法）。

## 5. **关键结论**

- **间接求解策略**：通过电势 V 间接求电场 $E = -\nabla V$，**避免直接积分库仑定律的复杂性**。
- **微分方程的优势**：泊松方程和拉普拉斯方程将积分问题转化为微分方程，更适合处理边界条件明确的问题（如导体电势固定的情况）。

In mathematics, it plays a major role in analytic function theory. To get a feel for Laplace’s equation and its solutions (which are called **harmonic functions**), we shall begin with the one- and two-dimensional versions, which are easier to picture, and illustrate all the essential properties of the three-dimensional case.

# 3.1.2 一维拉普拉斯方程 (Laplace's Equation in One Dimension)

## 1. **方程形式与通解**

**方程简化**：当电势 V 仅依赖于一维坐标 x 时，拉普拉斯方程退化为二阶常微分方程： 

$$\frac{d^2 V}{dx^2} = 0$$

**通解结构**：其通解为线性函数： 

$$V(x) = m x + b \tag{3.6}$$ ^b674f6

其中 m（斜率）和 b（截距）由边界条件唯一确定。

## 2. **边界条件的作用**

通过给定边界电势值（如 $V(x_1)$ 和 $V(x_2)$），可解出 m 和 b。例如，若 $V(1)=4$ 和 $V(5)=0$，则 $m=-1$，$b=5$，得到 $V(x) = -x + 5$ 。如 图3.1。

![[imgs/1-d-laplace-solution.png]]

> [!note] 
> I want to call your attention to two features of this result; they may seem silly and obvious in one dimension, where I can write down the general solution explicitly, but **the analogs in two and three dimensions are powerful and by no means obvious**

## 3. 性质

1. $V(x)$ is the average of $V(x+a)$ and $V(x-a)$ , for any $a :$ $$V(x)=\frac{1}{2}[V(x+a)+V(x-a)] $$Laplace’s equation is a kind of averaging instruction; it tells you to **assign to the point x the average of the values to the left and to the right of x** . Solutions to Laplace’s equation are, in this sense, as boring as they could possibly be, and yet fit the end points properly.
   
   这表明拉普拉斯方程的解是 “**平滑**” 的，**无局部剧烈变化**。

2. Laplace’s equation tolerates **no local maxima or minima**; **extreme values of V must occur at the end points**. Actually, this is a consequence of item 1, for **if there were a local maximum, V would be greater at that point than on either side, and therefore could not be the average**. (Ordinarily, you expect the second derivative to be negative at a maximum and positive at a minimum. Since Laplace’s equation requires, on the contrary, that the second derivative is zero, it seems reasonable that solutions should exhibit no extrema. However, this is not a proof, since there exist functions that have maxima and minima at points where the second derivative vanishes: $x^{4}$ , for example, at the point $x=0$)
   
   解在区间 **内部无最大值或最小值**，所有 **极值仅出现在边界**。这是因为若存在局部极值，$V(x)$ 将无法满足 **平均值特性**

# 3.1.3 二维拉普拉斯方程 (Laplace’s Equation in Two Dimensions)

## 1. 解的形式

If V depends on two variables, Laplace’s equation becomes $$\frac{\partial^{2} V}{\partial x^{2}}+\frac{\partial^{2} V}{\partial y^{2}}=0 .$$
This is no **longer an ordinary differential equation** (that is, one involving ordinary derivatives only); it’s a **partial differential equation** .

Indeed, one **cannot write down a “general solution”** (at least, not in a closed form like [[3.1 Laplace's Equation#^b674f6|Eq 3.6]]). Nevertheless, it is possible to deduce certain properties common to all solutions.

该方程是二阶偏微分方程，**通解无法用简单的闭合形式表示**，但具有特定的物理和数学性质。

It may help to have a physical example in mind. Picture a **thin rubber sheet (or a soap film)** stretched over some support. For definiteness, suppose you take a **cardboard box, cut a wavy line all the way around**, and **remove the top part (Fig. 3.2**). **Now glue a tightly stretched rubber membrane over the box**, so that it fits like a drum head (it won’t be a flat drumhead, of course, unless you chose to cut the edges off straight). 

假设有一个纸板箱，顶部被切割成波浪形边缘（图 3.2），然后用一张紧绷的橡胶膜覆盖在箱子上，形成类似鼓面的结构。

![[imgs/2-d-laplace-solution.png]]

Now, if you lay out coordinates $(x, y)$ on the bottom of the box, the **height** $V(x, y)$ of the sheet above the point $(x, y)$ will **satisfy Laplace’s equation**. (The onedimensional analog would be a rubber band stretched between two points. Of course, it would form a straight line.)

## 2. 解的特性

1. The value of V at a point $(x, y)$ is **the average of those around the point**. More precisely, if you **draw a circle of any radius R about the point $(x, y)$** , the **average value of V on the circle is equal to the value at the center**: $$V(x, y)=\frac{1}{2 \pi R} \oint_{circle } V d l .$$
   任意点 $(x, y)$ 处的电势等于其 **周围任意半径 R 的圆形区域** 的 **平均值**
   
   (This, incidentally, suggests the **method of relaxation**, on which many computer solutions to Laplace’s equation are based: **Starting with specified values for V at the boundary**, and reasonable **guesses for V on a grid of interior points**, the first pass reassigns to each point the average of its nearest neighbors. The second pass repeats the process, using the corrected values, and so on. **After a few iterations, the numbers begin to settle down**, so that subsequent passes produce negligible changes, and a numerical solution to Laplace’s equation, with the given boundary values, has been achieved.)
   
   **松弛法**：基于平均值特性，松弛法通过迭代更新网格点电势为相邻点的平均值，逐步逼近解。初始猜测后，反复迭代直至收敛（段落）

2. As a consequence, **V can have no local maxima or minima**; the **extreme values of V must occur at the boundaries**. (For **if V had a local maximum at r**, then by the very nature of maximum I could draw a sphere around r over which all values of V – and **a fortiori the average – would be less than at $r.$** ) 
   
   解在**区域内部无最大值或最小值**，所有极值仅出现在边界。这是因为若存在局部极值，$V(x, y)$ 将无法满足平均值特性。

# 3.1.4 三维拉普拉斯方程 (Laplace's Equation in Three Dimensions)

三维拉普拉斯方程没有具体显式的解，也很难举出实际的例子，但其方程 $\nabla^{2}V = 0$ 的解仍然满足以下两个性质：

1. The value of V at point r is **the average value of V over a spherical surface** of radius R centered at r $$V(r)=\frac{1}{4 \pi R^{2}} \oint_{sphere } V d a .$$
   
   某一点的电势的值 **等于周围一个球壳的电势的平均值** 。

2. V can **have no local maxima or minima**; the **extreme values of V must occur at the boundaries**. (For if V had a local maximum at r, then by the very nature of maximum I could draw a sphere around r over which all values of V – and a fortiori the average – would be less than at r
   
   $V$ 在该区域内 **没有最大值也没有最小值**，**最值只在边界取得**
   
   *Proof*. Let’s begin by calculating the **average potential over a spherical surface** of radius R due to a single point charge q located outside the sphere. We might as well center the sphere at the origin and choose coordinates so that q lies on the z-axis (Fig. 3.3). The potential at a point on the surface is $$V=\frac{1}{4 \pi \varepsilon_{0}} \frac{q}{r},$$ where $$r^{2}=z^{2}+R^{2}-2 z R cos \theta$$ (by the **law of cosines**), so $$\begin{aligned} V_{ave } & =\frac{1}{4 \pi R^{2}} \frac{q}{4 \pi \varepsilon_{0}} \int\left[z^{2}+R^{2}-2 z R cos \theta\right]^{-1 / 2} R^{2} sin \theta d \theta d \phi \\ & =\left.\frac{q}{4 \pi \varepsilon_{0}} \frac{1}{2 z R} \sqrt{z^{2}+R^{2}-2 z R cos \theta}\right|_{0} ^{\pi} \\ & =\frac{q}{4 \pi \varepsilon_{0}} \frac{1}{2 z R}[(z+R)-(z-R)]=\frac{1}{4 \pi \varepsilon_{0}} \frac{q}{z} . \end{aligned}$$
   But this is precisely the **potential due to q at the center of the sphere**! By the superposition principle, the same goes for any collection of charges outside the sphere: **their average potential over the sphere is equal to the net potential they produce at the center**. 
   
   周围区域的电势的积分等于中心点的电势

# 3.1.5 边界条件与唯一性定理 (Boundary Conditions and Uniqueness Theorems)

Laplace’s equation **does not by itself determine V** ; in addition, **suitable boundary conditions must be imposed**.

拉普拉斯方程本身无法唯一确定电势 V，需结合边界条件。关键问题是：**如何选择边界条件以确保解的唯一性？**

We might, for instance, **specify the value of the function at each end**, or we might give the **value of the function and its derivative at one end**, or the value at one end and the derivative at the other, and so on. 

But we **cannot get away with just the value or just the derivative at one end** – this is insufficient information. **Nor would it do to specify the derivatives at both ends** – this would **either be redundant (if the two are equal) or inconsistent (if they are not)** 

一阶方程需两个边界条件（如两端电势值、一端电势和导数）; 二维 / 三维情况需指定区域边界上的电势值（第一类边界条件）或其法向导数（第二类边界条件），或混合条件。

In two or three dimensions we are confronted by a partial differential equation, and it is not so obvious what would constitute acceptable boundary conditions. 

The answer, as I think your intuition would suggest, is that **V is uniquely determined by its value at the boundary**.

The proof that **a proposed set of boundary conditions will suffice** is usually presented in the form of a **uniqueness theorem.** 

> [!note] First Uniqueness Theorem. #therom/Electrodynamics/potentials 
> The solution to Laplace’s equation in some volume $\mathcal{V}$ is uniquely determined if $V$ is **specified on the boundary surface** $\mathcal{S}$ .
> 
> 在区域 $\mathcal{V}$ 内，若电势 $V$ 满足拉普拉斯方程且在边界 $\mathcal{S}$ 上取给定值，则 $V$ 唯一确定。

- **证明思路**：
    1. 假设存在两个解 $V_1$ 和 $V_2$，定义差 $V_3 = V_1 - V_2$。
    2. $V_3$ 满足拉普拉斯方程且在边界上为零。
    3. 由拉普拉斯方程的极值性质，$V_3$ 在内部无法取极值，故 $V_3 \equiv 0$，即 $V_1 = V_2$。

> [!note] Proof
> In Fig. 3.5 I have drawn such a region and its boundary. (There could also be “islands” inside, so long as V is given on all their surfaces; also, the outer boundary could be at infinity, where V is ordinarily taken to be zero.) **Suppose there were two solutions to Laplace’s equation**: $$\nabla^{2} V_{1}=0 \qquad and \qquad \nabla^{2} V_{2}=0 ,$$
> 
> ![[imgs/fig3.5-proof-of-1st-unique.png|400]]
> 
> both of which assume the specified value on the surface. I want to prove that they must be equal. The trick is look at their **difference**: $$V_{3} \equiv V_{1}-V_{2} .$$ 
> This obeys Laplace’s equation, $$\nabla^{2} V_{3}=\nabla^{2} V_{1}-\nabla^{2} V_{2}=0,$$ 
> and it **takes the value zero on all boundaries** (since $V_{1}$ and $V_{2}$ are equal there).
> 
> 在边界上两个解 $V_{1}, V_{2}$ 的值是相等的，否则边界条件不一致
> 
> But Laplace’s equation allows no local maxima or minima – all extrema occur on the boundaries. So the maximum and minimum of $V_{3}$ are both zero. Therefore $V_{3}$ must be zero everywhere, and hence $$V_{1}=V_{2} .$$ 

> [!example] Example 3.1
> Show that the potential is constant inside an enclosure completely surrounded by a conductor, provided there is no charge within the enclosure.
> 
> *Solution*. The potential on the cavity wall is some constant, $V_{0}$ (**导体表面等势**), so the potential inside is a function that **satisfies Laplace’s equation and has the constant value $V_{0}$ at the boundary**. It doesn’t take a genius to think of one solution to this problem: $V=V_{0}$ everywhere. **The uniqueness theorem guarantees that this is the only solution**. (It follows that the field inside an empty cavity is zero – 导体空腔内部电场处处为0)

The uniqueness theorem is a **license to your imagination**. It **doesn’t matter how you come by your solution**; if 

1. it satisfies Laplace’s equation and 
2. it has the correct value on the boundaries, 

**then it’s right**. You’ll see the power of this argument when we come to the [[method of images]].

Incidentally, it is easy to improve on the first uniqueness theorem: I assumed there was no charge inside the region in question, so the potential obeyed Laplace’s equation, **but we may as well throw in some charge (in which case V obeys Poisson’s equation)**. The argument is the same, only this time $$\nabla^{2} V_{1}=-\frac{1}{\varepsilon_{0}} \rho, \nabla^{2} V_{2}=-\frac{1}{\varepsilon_{0}} \rho,$$
so $$\nabla^{2} V_{3}=\nabla^{2} V_{1}-\nabla^{2} V_{2}=-\frac{1}{\varepsilon_{0}} \rho+\frac{1}{\varepsilon_{0}} \rho=0 .$$
Once again the **difference** $(V_{3} \equiv V_{1}-V_{2})$ ) **satisfies Laplace’s equation and has the value zero on all boundaries, so $V_{3}=0$ and hence $V_{1}=V_{2}$ .**

> [!note] Corollary
> The potential in a volume $\mathcal{V}$ is **uniquely determined** if 
> 1. the **charge density throughout the region**, and 
> 2. the v**alue of $V$ on all boundaries, are specified**.

# 3.1.6 导体与第二唯一性定理 (Conductors and the Second Uniqueness Theorem)

In the laboratory, we have conductors **connected to batteries**, which **maintain a given potential**, or **to ground**, which is **the experimentalist’s word for V = 0.** 

However, there are other circumstances in which **we do not know the potential** at the boundary, but **rather the charges on various conducting surfaces**. Suppose I **put charge Qa on the first conductor, Qb on the second**, and so on – I’m **not telling you how the charge distributes itself over each conducting surface**, because **as soon as I put it on, it moves around in a way I do not control.**

在静电学中，导体表面的电荷分布往往未知，但实验中常 **通过控制导体的总电荷（而非具体分布）来研究电场**。此时，如何保证电场的唯一性？

And for good measure, let’s say there is some specified charge density ρ in the region between the conductors. Is the electric field now uniquely determined? Or are there perhaps a number of different ways the charges could arrange themselves on their respective conductors, each leading to a different field?

如果某个区域内有特定的电荷密度，那我们能够确定电场或电势吗？

> [!note] Second Uniqueness Theorem #therom/Electrodynamics/potentials 
> In a volume $\mathcal{V}$ **surrounded by conductors and containing a specified charge density** $\rho$ , the electric field is **uniquely determined if the total charge on each conductor is given** (Fig. 3.6). (The region as a whole can be bounded by another conductor, or else unbounded.)
> 
> 在 **由导体包围的区域** $\mathcal{V}$ 内，若 **电荷密度 $\rho$ 已知**，且 **每个导体的总电荷 $Q_i$ 给定**，则区域内的 **电场 $E$ 唯一确定** ，进而 **电势唯一确定**。
> 
> ![[imgs/2nd-unique.png|400]]

- **证明思路**
	- **假设存在两个解**：设 $E_1$ 和 $E_2$ 均满足高斯定律和导体表面的边界条件。
	- **构造差值场**：定义 $E_3 = E_1 - E_2$，则 $E_3$ 满足：
	    1. $\nabla \cdot E_3 = 0$（区域内无额外电荷）。
	    2. $\oint E_3 \cdot da = 0$（导体表面总通量为零）。
	- **能量法证明**：通过格林定理证明 $E_3$ 的能量密度积分为零，因此 $E_3 \equiv 0$，即 $E_1 = E_2$

> [!note] Proof
> **Suppose there are two fields** satisfying the conditions of the problem. Both **obey Gauss’s law** in differential form in the space between the conductors: $$\nabla \cdot E_{1}=\frac{1}{\varepsilon_{0}} \rho, \nabla \cdot E_{2}=\frac{1}{\varepsilon_{0}} \rho .$$
> 
> And both obey Gauss’s law in integral form for a Gaussian surface enclosing each conductor:  $$\oint_{\substack{i th conducting \\ surface }} E_{1} \cdot d a=\frac{1}{\varepsilon_{0}} Q_{i}, \oint_{\substack{i th conducting \\ surface }} E_{2} \cdot d a=\frac{1}{\varepsilon_{0}} Q_{i} .$$
> 
> Likewise, for the **outer boundary** (whether this is just inside an enclosing conductor or at infinity), $$\oint_{\substack{ outer \\ boundary }} E_{1} \cdot d a=\frac{1}{\varepsilon_{0}} Q_{tot }, \oint_{\substack{ outer \\ boundary }} E_{2} \cdot d a=\frac{1}{\varepsilon_{0}} Q_{tot } .$$
> 
> As before, we examine the difference $$E_{3} \equiv E_{1}-E_{2},$$ which obeys $$\nabla \cdot E_{3} = \nabla \cdot E_{1} - \nabla \cdot E_{2} = \dfrac{\rho}{\varepsilon_{0}} - \dfrac{\rho}{\varepsilon_{0}} = 0 \tag{3.7}$$ in the region between the conductors, and $$\oint E_{3} \cdot d a=0 \tag{3.8}$$ over each boundary surface.
> 
> Now there is one final piece of information we must exploit: 
> Although **we do not know how the charge $Q_{i}$ distributes itself over the ith conductor**, **we *do* know that each conductor is an equipotential**, and hence **$V_{3}$ is a constant (not necessarily the same constant) over each conducting surface**. (It need not be zero, for the potentials $V_{1}$ and $V_{2}$ may not be equal – all we know for sure is that both are constant over any given conductor.) 
> 
> 对于内部的导体，其表面的电势一定为某个常数，这是因为导体为等势体
> 
> Next comes a trick. Invoking [[../01 Vector Analysis/1.2 Differential Calculus#^509665|divergence product rule]] (inside front cover), we find that $$\nabla \cdot\left(V_{3} E_{3}\right)=V_{3}\left(\nabla \cdot E_{3}\right)+E_{3} \cdot\left(\nabla V_{3}\right) = 0 + E_{3} (-E_{3}) =-\left(E_{3}\right)^{2} .$$
> 
> Here I have used Eq 3.7 , and $E_{3}=-\nabla V_{3}$ . Integrating this over $\mathcal{V}$ , and applying the [[../01 Vector Analysis/1.3 Integral Calculus#^497ee6|divergence theorem]] to the left-hand side, $$\int_{\mathcal{V}} \nabla \cdot\left(V_{3} E_{3}\right) d \tau=\oint_{\mathcal{S}} V_{3} E_{3} \cdot d a=-\int_{\mathcal{V}}\left(E_{3}\right)^{2} d \tau .$$
> 
> The surface **integral covers all boundaries of the region** in question – the **conductors and outer boundary**. Now $V_{3}$ is a **constant** over each surface (if the outer boundary is infinity, $V_{3}=0$ there), so it **comes outside each integral**, and what remains is zero, according to Eq. 3.8. Therefore, $$\int_{\mathcal{V}}\left(E_{3}\right)^{2} d \tau=0$$
> 
> But this integrand is never negative; the only way the integral can vanish is if $E_{3}=0$ everywhere. Consequently, $E_{1}=E_{2}$ , and the theorem is proved. 
> 
> 这个积分永远没有负数出现，因此想要积分为零，只能是 $E_{3} = 0$ 。

# Summary

- **引言**（Introduction）
	- **核心任务**：通过电势 V 间接求解电场 $E = -\nabla V$。
	- **泊松方程**：$\nabla^2 V = -\frac{\rho}{\epsilon_0}$，描述有电荷区域的电势。
	- **拉普拉斯方程**：$\nabla^2 V = 0$（无电荷区域），在笛卡尔坐标系中展开为： $\frac{\partial^2 V}{\partial x^2} + \frac{\partial^2 V}{\partial y^2} + \frac{\partial^2 V}{\partial z^2} = 0$
	- **适用场景**：导体内部、无电荷区域的电势分析。

- **一维拉普拉斯方程**（Laplace’s Equation in One Dimension）
	- **方程形式**：$\frac{d^2 V}{dx^2} = 0$，通解为线性函数 $V(x) = mx + b$。
	- **边界条件**：需两个条件（如端点电势）确定 m 和 b。
	- **解的性质**：
	    - **平均值特性**：$V(x) = \frac{1}{2}[V(x+a) + V(x-a)]$。
	    - **无局部极值**：极值仅出现在边界。

-  **二维拉普拉斯方程**（Laplace’s Equation in Two Dimensions）
	- **方程形式**：$\frac{\partial^2 V}{\partial x^2} + \frac{\partial^2 V}{\partial y^2} = 0$。
	- **物理模型**：橡胶膜或肥皂膜的平衡形状满足该方程。
	- **解的性质**：
	    - **平均值特性**：任意点电势等于其周围圆形区域的平均值。
	    - **无局部极值**：极值仅出现在边界。
	- **数值方法**：松弛法通过迭代更新网格点电势为相邻点的平均值。

- **三维拉普拉斯方程**（Laplace’s Equation in Three Dimensions）
	- **方程形式**：$\nabla^2 V = 0$，无显式通解。
	- **解的性质**：
	    - **平均值特性**：任意点电势等于其周围球形区域的平均值。
	    - **无局部极值**：极值仅出现在边界。
	- **证明方法**：利用高斯定理和对称性分析。
- **边界条件与唯一性定理**（Boundary Conditions and Uniqueness Theorems）
	- **边界条件类型**：
	    - **第一类**：边界电势已知（Dirichlet 条件）。
	    - **第二类**：边界法向导数已知（Neumann 条件）。
	- **第一唯一性定理**：
	    - **陈述**：区域内电势由边界电势唯一确定。
	    - **证明**：假设两解之差 $V_3 = V_1 - V_2$，利用极值性质证其恒为零。
	- **应用**：导体空腔内无电荷时，电势恒等于边界值。

- **导体与第二唯一性定理**（Conductors and the Second Uniqueness Theorem）
	- **第二唯一性定理**：
	    - **陈述**：若导体总电荷和区域电荷密度已知，则电场唯一确定。
	    - **证明**：构造差值场 $E_3 = E_1 - E_2$，利用格林定理证其能量密度积分为零。
	- **物理意义**：允许通过控制导体总电荷唯一确定电场，无需知道电荷具体分布。
	- **应用**：解释导体球对的电荷重新分布、静电屏蔽等现象。
